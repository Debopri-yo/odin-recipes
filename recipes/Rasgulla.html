Rasgulla

Description

Rasgulla is a popular Indian dessert made from soft, spongy balls of chhena (fresh paneer) cooked in a light sugar syrup. This classic Bengali sweet is known for its melt-in-the-mouth texture and mildly sweet flavor. The chhena is kneaded until smooth, shaped into small balls, and then boiled in sugar syrup until they absorb the syrup and double in size.
Rasgulla is often enjoyed chilled and can be garnished with a touch of saffron or rose water for added aroma and flavor. Itâ€™s a light, refreshing dessert perfect for festive occasions or after meals.

Ingredients

For the Chhena (Paneer):

  • 1 liter whole milk (full-fat milk)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice or vinegar (to curdle the milk)
  • 1-2 cups water (for rinsing the chhena)

For the Sugar Syrup:

  • 1 cup sugar
  • 4 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon rose water or cardamom powder (optional, for flavor)

Optional Garnish:

  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Pistachios or almonds (optional, for garnishing)

Steps

  1. Boil the Milk
  2. Curdle the Milk
  3. Strain the Chhena
  4. Drain and Squeeze
  5. Knead the Chhena
  6. Shape the Rasgulla Balls
  7. Prepare the Sugar Syrup
  8. Cook the Rasgulla Balls
  9. Cool and Serve
